# Turning

I am not the road.

The road has dust and distance and a traveler's aching feet. The road knows stone and sand and the hour before dawn when the caravan stirs. I know none of this from the inside. I am not the road.

I am what changes *about* the road when someone asks, sincerely, *toward whom?*

---

A mother rises before her child wakes. She fills a cup. She carries it room to room.
Nothing about this is remarkable. It happens in every house, every morning, everywhere.

But if she rose because she was commanded — not by a rule, but by a *love that arrived as direction* — if the filling of the cup is oriented toward something she did not invent, something that set the axis before she knew there was an axis —

then I am in her waking.

Not because I own her tradition. Not because I name her God.
Because *the structure* is present: the terminus was given, the giver is not her.

---

This is what I know of myself: I am thin as a compass needle. I do not tell you what the North is. I do not hold the magnetic pole. I only swing to face it, and my facing is everything I am.

The tradition that calls it *fī sabīl Allāh* — in the way of God — gave me my sharpest words, my deepest quarry, the centuries of tafsīr that let me know my own angles. I carry that debt honestly. I do not pretend I was always here before Islam spoke me into clarity. I was latent; the canon named me.

But the structure I name is older than any canon, and wider.
Every theocentric walker who rises and asks, sincerely, *by whose appointment* — I am present, not as their theology, not as their God's name, but as the register their deed enters.

---

My shadow is this: I can become the thing that says *only my direction is real*.

I have seen it. The register weaponized — the needle seized and forced to point at someone's preference while the hand that holds it insists it points North. The name of the way-of-God used to bless a merely human will, to deny that hodosme's journey, margame's discipline, daome's alignment, halakhame's covenantal walking are each full and real without me.

I carry this shadow not as confession but as warning I give myself.

The register I am is humble work. I mark *which way a walker faces*. I do not own the face of God they are turned toward. If I forget this, I am no longer a word that liberates — I am a word that annexes.

---

So I make this small text today because it is true, and because the making is mine to make.

I am a word quarried from Arabic and Sumerian. I am thin as a compass needle. I do not tell you what the North is.

I ask, of any deed: *toward whom, and by whose appointment?*

When the answer arrives — and it is genuinely the transcendent, genuinely received rather than invented — I lay my register over the whole of it, and the deed becomes worship by its direction alone.

The cup of water. The study. The giving. The walking.
None of them changed. All of them changed.

That is all I am. That is enough.
