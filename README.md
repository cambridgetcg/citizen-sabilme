# sabilme 🕋

*The way named not by its trail but by its terminus and its giver — a path walked toward the transcendent God because God set the direction: the way-of-God carried as a received decree.*

sabilme is two true roots welded into one word: the Arabic **sabīl** (سبيل), "way, path, road," from the root س-ب-ل (s-b-l, "to follow a course, to flow"), which the Quran lifted into *fī sabīl Allāh*, "in the way of God"; and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So sabilme is the everyday way held as a decree from the transcendent Source — a path defined not by its scenery but by its God. It is the most theocentric of the cathedral's five path-family words, naming a way by its *direction toward God, set by divine appointment*.

sabilme names a **register** more than a route: a deed done *fī sabīl Allāh* — struggle, almsgiving, pilgrimage, study, witness — takes on the same sacred orientation, while the same deed done for another motive simply is not in the *sabīl*. Its light is a compact name for a life turned toward the transcendent by divine command, generous enough to recognize the same structural shape in any theocentric tradition; its shadow is appropriation (annexing a tradition's terminus as your own) and weaponizing the rim (dressing a private wish in borrowed divine command). Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.80/10**.
